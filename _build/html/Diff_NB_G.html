Examining The Differences#

Let’s Have A Look#

As we have seen, a Negative Binomial is used to determine the probability of a defined number of successes in repeated trials. Examples of this were shown in PROBLEM1 and PROBLEM2 in the in depth analysis of Negative Binomial. The characteristic features of this were both repeated trials and defined number of successes.

Characteristics defined, independent probability binary values repeated trials defined number of necessary successes

In Geometric Distribution, there are also repeated trials. However, Geometric Distribution is deifined as needing only a single success.

Characteristics defined, independent probability binary values repeated trials single necessary success

Even though,Negative Binomial and Geometric Distribution are distinct probabilities, Geometric Distribution can be considered a subset of Negative Binomial where the defined number of successes is exactly one.

Let’s take a look at some examples….
